## Executive Summary

The Arrakis content calendar is designed for a **bootstrapped team** - high-impact, low-volume content that compounds over time. Focus on evergreen educational content and authentic community engagement over high-frequency posting.

**Content Philosophy:**
> Quality over quantity. One exceptional piece beats ten forgettable ones. Compound by teaching, not just announcing.

**Content Pillars:**
1. **Conviction Intelligence** - Why engagement matters beyond access
2. **Community Building** - Strategies for Web3 communities
3. **Product Education** - How to use Arrakis features
4. **Credibility & Trust** - Dune expertise, case studies

## Content Templates

### Twitter Thread Template

**Length:** 8-12 tweets

**Structure:**
1. Hook (controversial or surprising statement)
2. Problem statement
3. Why it matters
4. Solution/insight
5-8. Supporting points with examples
9. Summary/takeaway
10. CTA + engagement ask

**Example Hook Formulas:**
- "Most DAOs get this wrong about token-gating..."
- "I analyzed 50 token-gated communities. Here's what separates winners from losers..."
- "Your token holders aren't your community. Here's the difference..."

### Blog Post Template

**Length:** 1,500-2,500 words

**Structure:**
```markdown
# [Compelling Title]

## TL;DR
[3-5 bullet points summarizing key takeaways]

## The Problem
[Set up the pain point]

## Why It Matters
[Stakes and implications]

## The Solution
[Your insight or approach]

## How to Apply This
[Actionable steps]

## Examples
[Real-world illustrations]

## Conclusion
[Recap + CTA]
```

### Case Study Template

**Length:** 1,000-1,500 words

**Structure:**
```markdown
# How [Community] [Achievement] with Arrakis

## Quick Facts
- Community: [Name]
- Size: [Members]
- Challenge: [Problem]
- Result: [Outcome]

## The Challenge
[Before state, pain points]

## Why They Chose Arrakis
[Decision factors]

## The Implementation
[How they set up, timeline]

## The Results
[Metrics, outcomes, quotes]

## Key Takeaways
[Lessons for readers]

## Try It Yourself
[CTA]
```

## Channel-Specific Guidelines

### Twitter/X

**Posting Schedule:**
- Threads: 2-3x per week (Tue/Thu/Sat)
- Engagement: Daily
- Quick insights: 3-5x per week

**Best Practices:**
- Tweet at 8-10 AM ET, 2-4 PM ET
- Use threads for substance, singles for engagement
- Reply to mentions within 2 hours
- QRT with value-add, not just praise
- Use visuals (charts, graphics) when possible

**Voice:**
- Confident but not arrogant
- Technical but accessible
- Direct, no fluff
- Occasionally contrarian

### Blog/Website

**Posting Schedule:**
- Long-form: 1x per week
- Quick updates: As needed

**SEO Targets:**
- "token gating alternatives"
- "discord community tools"
- "dao community management"
- "nft holder engagement"
- "web3 community analytics"

**Content Mix:**
- 40% Educational (how-to, guides)
- 30% Thought leadership (insights, opinions)
- 20% Social proof (case studies, testimonials)
- 10% Product (announcements, features)

### Discord

**Posting Schedule:**
- Announcements: As needed
- Community posts: Daily
- Support: Continuous

**Channel Structure:**
- #announcements - Official updates only
- #general - Community discussion
- #support - Help and troubleshooting
- #showcase - User success stories
- #feedback - Feature requests

### YouTube

**Posting Schedule:**
- Tutorial videos: Bi-weekly

**Video Types:**
- Quick starts (5 min)

## Content Production Workflow

### Weekly Content Workflow

```
Monday: Plan week's content
        ↓
Tuesday-Wednesday: Create hero content
        ↓
Thursday: Review and edit
        ↓
Friday: Schedule and prepare
        ↓
Weekend: Engage and respond
```

### Content Creation Process

1. **Ideation** - Draw from pillar topics, user questions, market trends
2. **Outline** - Structure before writing
3. **Draft** - Write fast, edit slow
4. **Review** - Technical accuracy check
5. **Polish** - Headlines, formatting, visuals
6. **Schedule** - Queue in scheduling tool
7. **Promote** - Cross-channel amplification
8. **Measure** - Track performance

### Tools

| Purpose | Tool | Cost |
|---------|------|------|
| Scheduling | Buffer or Typefully | $15-30/mo |
| Graphics | Canva | Free-$13/mo |
| Writing | Notion | Free |
| Analytics | Twitter Analytics, Plausible | Free-$9/mo |
| Video | Loom + iMovie | Free-$15/mo |
