# Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs)

**Research Date:** 2026-01-03
**Analyst:** Market Research (GTM Collective)
**Status:** Complete

---

## Executive Summary

Based on market research and product capabilities, Arrakis has three primary ICP segments with distinct needs, pain points, and buying patterns. The highest-value segment is established DAOs/NFT projects seeking to differentiate through engagement, not just access control.

**ICP Priority:**
1. **ICP-1: The Engagement-Focused DAO** - Highest value, longest retention
2. **ICP-2: The Growing NFT Project** - Volume opportunity, viral potential
3. **ICP-3: The DeFi Protocol** - High ACV, technical buyers

---

## ICP-1: The Engagement-Focused DAO

### Profile Summary

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Organization Type** | Established DAO (12+ months old) |
| **Community Size** | 5,000-50,000 Discord members |
| **Treasury Size** | $500K-$10M |
| **Team Size** | 5-20 contributors |
| **Current Tools** | Collab.Land + MEE6 + custom scripts |
| **Technical Level** | Non-technical community leads |

### Firmographics

**Industries:**
- Protocol governance DAOs (DeFi, infrastructure)
- Investment/venture DAOs
- Social/community DAOs
- Media/content DAOs

**Geography:**
- Global, English-speaking primary
- NA (40%), EU (30%), APAC (20%), Other (10%)

**Funding:**
- Token launch completed
- Treasury-funded operations
- May have received grants

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Severity | Frequency |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| "Can't identify who our real community is vs flippers" | Critical | Daily |
| "Token-gating alone doesn't drive engagement" | High | Weekly |
| "Managing 3-4 Discord bots is a nightmare" | Medium | Weekly |
| "Airdrops go to farmers, not believers" | Critical | Per event |
| "No visibility into holder conviction" | High | Daily |
| "Discord feels flat - everyone looks the same" | Medium | Ongoing |

### Jobs To Be Done

1. **Identify true believers** - Distinguish diamond hands from traders
2. **Reward long-term holders** - Create differentiated experiences by commitment
3. **Drive governance participation** - Engaged members vote more
4. **Prepare fair airdrops** - Distribute to contributors, not farmers
5. **Reduce operational overhead** - Consolidate bot management

### Buying Behavior

**Decision Makers:**
- Community Lead / DAO Operations
- Core contributor team (3-5 people)
- Sometimes token vote for significant expenses

**Buying Triggers:**
- Upcoming airdrop or token event
- Community engagement declining
- Frustration with current tool stack
- Competitor DAO adopted better tools

**Evaluation Criteria:**
1. Can it identify our most valuable members?
2. Does it integrate with what we have?
3. What's the migration risk?
4. Is pricing predictable?
5. Will our community trust it?

**Sales Cycle:**
- Discovery to trial: 1-2 weeks
- Trial to decision: 2-4 weeks
- Decision to rollout: 1-2 weeks
- **Total: 4-8 weeks**

**Budget:**
- Premium tier: $99-149/mo (treasury expense)
- Enterprise: $399-499/mo (larger DAOs)
- Budget approval: Usually unilateral for <$500/mo

### Messaging

**Value Proposition:**
> "Know your community, not just your holders. Arrakis transforms your Discord from an access-controlled chat to a progression-driven experience that rewards your most committed members."

**Key Messages:**
- "Find your diamond hands before your next airdrop"
- "9-tier progression creates FOMO that drives engagement"
- "Shadow mode means zero risk - try alongside Collab.Land"
- "Built by the #1 Dune team - we know on-chain data"

### Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already use Collab.Land" | "Perfect - Arrakis runs in shadow mode alongside it. See the value, then decide." |
| "Our community is used to current setup" | "Tiered progression adds to their experience, doesn't change what works." |
| "How do we know conviction scoring works?" | "We'll show you your data in shadow mode. You validate before committing." |
| "Pricing is higher than Collab.Land basic" | "You're not buying access control - you're buying engagement intelligence." |

## ICP-2: The Growing NFT Project

### Profile Summary

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Organization Type** | NFT collection (minted, active community) |
| **Community Size** | 1,000-20,000 Discord members |
| **Floor Price** | 0.1-5 ETH |
| **Team Size** | 2-10 people |
| **Current Tools** | Collab.Land or Guild.xyz |
| **Technical Level** | Artist/creator-led, minimal technical |

### Firmographics

**Project Types:**
- PFP collections
- Generative art projects
- Utility NFTs (membership, access)
- Gaming/metaverse land

**Geography:**
- Global, heavy Twitter/Discord presence
- NA (35%), EU (35%), APAC (25%), Other (5%)

**Funding:**
- Mint revenue funded
- Royalties sustaining (if marketplace supports)
- May be exploring utility pivots

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Severity | Frequency |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| "Can't tell traders from collectors" | High | Daily |
| "Engagement dropped after mint" | Critical | Ongoing |
| "Whales get same treatment as 1-NFT holders" | Medium | Daily |
| "No way to reward long-term holders" | High | Weekly |
| "Lost royalties mean we need member engagement more than ever" | Critical | Ongoing |
| "Community feels generic compared to top projects" | Medium | Ongoing |

### Jobs To Be Done

1. **Retain post-mint engagement** - Keep community active after hype dies
2. **Identify collectors vs flippers** - Know who to prioritize
3. **Create holder hierarchy** - Reward multi-NFT and long-term holders
4. **Drive secondary engagement** - Incentivize holding over selling
5. **Build unique culture** - Stand out from generic Discord servers

### Buying Behavior

**Decision Makers:**
- Founder/Artist (often sole decision maker)
- Community manager
- Sometimes DAO if established

**Buying Triggers:**
- Post-mint engagement drop
- Competitor project has better Discord experience
- Planning holder benefits or airdrops
- Floor price concerns driving retention focus

**Evaluation Criteria:**
1. Does it look cool? (Aesthetic matters to artists)
2. Will it impress our holders?
3. How fast can we set it up?
4. What's the cost vs mint revenue?

**Sales Cycle:**
- Discovery to trial: 1 week
- Trial to decision: 1-2 weeks
- Decision to rollout: Same day
- **Total: 2-4 weeks** (faster than DAOs)

**Budget:**
- Free tier attractive for early projects
- Premium: $99/mo (cost of business)
- Highly price-sensitive post bear market

### Messaging

**Value Proposition:**
> "Your mint was just the beginning. Arrakis turns your holder community into a tiered experience where your top collectors feel special and flippers become believers."

**Key Messages:**
- "9-tier Dune-themed progression (Naib, Fedaykin, Fremen...)"
- "Turn floor-watchers into diamond hands"
- "Reward your OGs automatically with conviction scoring"
- "15-minute setup, zero code required"

### Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We're a small project" | "Start free with BasicTheme. Upgrade when you grow." |
| "Our community is used to Collab.Land" | "Shadow mode - they won't even know until you're ready." |
| "We can't afford premium tools" | "Free tier included. Premium is less than one NFT sale/month." |
| "Sounds complicated" | "15-minute wizard, no code. We'll guide you." |

## ICP-3: The DeFi Protocol

### Profile Summary

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Organization Type** | DeFi protocol with active governance |
| **Community Size** | 10,000-100,000+ Discord members |
| **TVL** | $10M-$1B+ |
| **Team Size** | 10-50+ contributors |
| **Current Tools** | Multiple bots, often custom |
| **Technical Level** | Highly technical team |

### Firmographics

**Protocol Types:**
- DEXs and AMMs
- Lending/borrowing protocols
- Yield aggregators
- Derivatives platforms
- L2s and infrastructure

**Geography:**
- Global, often legally distributed teams
- Crypto-native jurisdictions

**Funding:**
- VC-backed or foundation-funded
- Token treasury for operations
- Revenue from protocol fees

### Pain Points

| Pain Point | Severity | Frequency |
|------------|----------|-----------|
| "Governance participation is too low" | Critical | Every vote |
| "Can't distinguish users from speculators" | High | Ongoing |
| "Our Discord is chaos with 50k+ members" | Medium | Daily |
| "Sybil attacks on airdrops cost us millions" | Critical | Per event |
| "No way to tier access by protocol usage" | High | Ongoing |
| "Security requirements for tooling" | Critical | Ongoing |

### Jobs To Be Done

1. **Drive governance participation** - Get real users to vote
2. **Prevent sybil attacks** - Identify genuine users for airdrops
3. **Create user hierarchy** - Reward heavy protocol users
4. **Maintain security standards** - Audit trail, compliance needs
5. **Scale community operations** - Handle massive Discord servers

### Buying Behavior

**Decision Makers:**
- Head of Community / DevRel
- Protocol foundation
- Sometimes core team security review

**Buying Triggers:**
- Upcoming token launch or airdrop
- Governance proposal about community tools
- Security incident with current tools
- Growth outpacing current tooling

**Evaluation Criteria:**
1. Security audit status?
2. Enterprise features (RLS, audit trail)?
3. API access for custom integrations?
4. Support response time?
5. Can it handle our scale?

**Sales Cycle:**
- Discovery to evaluation: 2-4 weeks
- Technical review: 2-4 weeks
- Security review: 2-4 weeks
- Procurement: 2-4 weeks
- **Total: 8-16 weeks** (longest cycle)

**Budget:**
- Enterprise tier: $399-999/mo
- Custom pricing for large protocols
- Budget not primary concern (security/features are)

### Messaging

**Value Proposition:**
> "Enterprise-grade community infrastructure for protocols that can't afford to get security wrong. Arrakis delivers on-chain intelligence with the audit trail your foundation requires."

**Key Messages:**
- "PostgreSQL RLS for complete data isolation"
- "Full audit trail for compliance"
- "Score Service powered by top Dune team"
- "Custom themes for your protocol brand"

### Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We need to security review" | "Happy to share architecture docs. RLS-based isolation, no shared tenant data." |
| "Can you handle our scale?" | "Built for 100k+ members per community, 1000+ concurrent tenants." |
| "We have custom requirements" | "Enterprise tier includes custom themes and API access." |
| "What about uptime?" | "Two-tier provider means core gating works even if Score is down." |

## ICP Prioritization Matrix

| ICP | ACV Potential | Sales Cycle | Market Size | Strategic Value | Priority |
|-----|---------------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| ICP-1: Engagement DAO | Medium ($1-2K/yr) | Medium (4-8wk) | Large | High (reference) | **#1** |
| ICP-2: Growing NFT | Low ($0-1K/yr) | Short (2-4wk) | Very Large | Medium (volume) | **#2** |
| ICP-3: DeFi Protocol | High ($5-10K/yr) | Long (8-16wk) | Medium | High (credibility) | **#3** |

### Prioritization Rationale

**ICP-1 (DAOs) is primary because:**
- Highest retention (treasury-funded, ongoing need)
- Best product-market fit (engagement focus)
- Reasonable sales cycle
- Strong reference value

**ICP-2 (NFT Projects) is secondary because:**
- Volume opportunity (thousands of projects)
- Viral potential (projects share tools)
- Fast sales cycle for rapid iteration
- But: higher churn, price sensitive

**ICP-3 (DeFi Protocols) is tertiary because:**
- Highest ACV but longest cycle
- Credibility signal if won
- Resource-intensive sales
- Best approached after product maturity
